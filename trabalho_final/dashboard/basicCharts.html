Abstract

Over the past decade, we have seen considerable growth in research into computational health technologies. However, few academic papers address the perspective and challenges reported by developers during the development of health solutions. This research aims to collect and analyze eHealth discussions from Stack Overflow (SO) to identify trends and challenges based on the developers' perspective. We use the KDD process and the methodology proposed by BANDEIRA et al. (2019). First, we make a query for tags of questions from the SO database. Then, we process the data and apply the LDA algorithm for topic modeling. We have identified 3469 discussions, of which 1765 are open discussions. The most discussed topics include Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine (DICOM), Electronic Health Records (EHR), and technical issues about the development of mobile apps. The data analyzed allowed us to identify trends primarily related to the use of mobile application development platforms, as well as strong demand for interoperability standards and stable and well-documented tools to support the development of healthcare systems and applications.
